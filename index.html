How the peg board is arranged

The board forms a triangle of staggered pegs, with each lower row one peg wider than the row above it. A ball released at the top has an even chance of moving left or right at each bounce, and that process repeats until it reaches the bottom row. The final pocket determines the payout.

Because each bounce is a simple left-or-right choice, balls naturally collect near the centre and only a few reach the outer edges. Mathematicians call this a Galton board, while casino players mostly see it as a neat balance between regular small results and rare larger hits. In casino thorfortune style games, the payout table is built around that same pattern.

Six useful terms to know

Pocket
The slot at the bottom of the board where the final multiplier is paid.
Rows
The number of peg lines the ball crosses; more rows mean more pockets and tighter odds.
Risk level
A preset that reshapes the prize table without changing the core game maths much.

The small gap between true odds and payout values that gives the operator its edge.
Provably fair
A system where hashes and seeds let players verify that a round was not altered.
Variance
The amount of swing around the average result, which is what the risk setting controls most.
